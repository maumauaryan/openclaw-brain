## Outreach Philosophy

**"High-level founders buy relief, not persuasion."**

### Core Rules
- Intelligent, observational, non-pushy
- Value-first → DM only after engagement
- Call is optional, never forced
- 5-touch outreach sequence (not spam)

---

## Outreach Process

### Step 1 — Engage Publicly First ✓
- Comment on recent post
- Signal understanding, not selling
- Add value or spark curiosity
- One thoughtful comment (NOT "great post!")

### Step 2 — Move to DM
- After engagement → send personalized DM
- Offer insight before offers
- Keep it observational, peer-to-peer

### Step 3 — Send Loom
- 80-8-20 Loom structure
- What I noticed → Why it matters → One recommendation
- No pitch, just analysis

## Re-Reach Sequences

### Scenario A — No Response After 3 Days
"Hey [Name] — sent a Loom your way a few days back. No pressure on timing, but wanted to make sure it landed in your inbox and not the request folder."

### Scenario B — Viewed Loom But Didn't Reply
"Saw you opened the video — appreciate the time. Curious what you thought. Even a 'not relevant right now' is useful feedback."

### Scenario C — Went Cold After Positive Reply
"Still thinking about it, or did something shift? Happy to go deeper on any of the points I raised — or just close the loop if it's not the right time."

### Scenario D — Said 'Not Now'
"Completely understand — timing matters. Mind if I check back in 30 days? I'll have a few new case studies by then that might be relevant." (Set CRM reminder for 30 days)

2. **Qualify** (10 min): Keep 3–5, remove rest
3. **Comment** (10 min): Public engagement first
